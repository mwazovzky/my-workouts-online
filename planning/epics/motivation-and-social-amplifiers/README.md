# Epic: Motivation and Social Amplifiers

Add supporting features that strengthen habit formation, delight, and recommendation potential after the core workout product is already strong.

## Why This Epic Exists

Motivation and social features can increase stickiness and product visibility, but they should come after the core workout, progress, and onboarding experiences are already reliable.

Current gaps this epic addresses:

- No reminders or nudges
- No achievement moments
- No lightweight sharing
- No coach or training-partner visibility
- No features designed specifically for recommendation and habit reinforcement

These gaps matter because they can amplify a strong product and help users keep the app in their routine between training sessions.

## Target Audience

Primary: mixed audience

Secondary: users who respond strongly to routine reinforcement or social accountability

## Expected Product Diff

Before this epic:

- the app is mostly a personal utility tool
- habit reinforcement is weak outside the workout and review loops
- recommendation potential is limited because the product does not create many shareable or memorable moments

After this epic:

- the app provides more reasons to return between workouts
- users get lightweight encouragement and accountability
- the product becomes more likely to be talked about and shared

## Scope

This epic includes:

- reminders and nudges
- achievement moments
- lightweight sharing
- coach or training-partner visibility

## Non-Goals

This epic does not include:

- full community feed
- public social network features
- competitive ranking systems as a first step
- replacing the core product with gamification

## Candidate User Stories

1. Workout reminders
2. Weekly consistency nudges
3. Achievement moments for milestones or PRs
4. Lightweight workout or progress sharing
5. Private accountability view for a coach or partner

## Sequencing Rationale

- Start with reminders and achievement moments because they support retention with lower product complexity.
- Add lightweight sharing next if the product is already generating meaningful summary moments.
- Leave coach or partner visibility until privacy and value boundaries are clearer.

## Success Metrics

Primary signals:

- increased between-workout app opens
- improved weekly consistency
- increased retention among active users

Secondary signals:

- reminder opt-in rate
- achievement interaction rate
- sharing usage rate

## Risks

- social features can distract from the real utility of the product
- weak or noisy reminders can become annoying
- sharing features can feel forced if users do not see enough value in the underlying product

## Done Criteria

This epic is done when:

- motivation features strengthen return behavior without feeling spammy
- any social layer remains lightweight and clearly valuable
- relevant current-state docs are updated
- the added features amplify rather than distort the core product direction
